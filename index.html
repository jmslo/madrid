El Rastro
Flea Market Don't Miss
La Latina · Plaza de Cascorro to Ronda de Toledo · Every Sunday + public holidays · 9am–3pm · Free
Over 1,000 merchants sell from around 9am until roughly 3pm. The market has its hub in Plaza de Cascorro and spreads across a large triangular block, spilling down side streets. Depending on which section you pass through, you'll find antiques, vintage clothing, accessories, kitchenware, second-hand records, books, and objects of all kinds. The surrounding La Latina tapas bars are part of the experience — locals treat it as a whole Sunday morning. Arrive before 10am to actually move. After 11am it becomes a crowd management exercise.
⚑ Your Sundays: Apr 5, 12 (skip Apr 19 — retrieval day)
Mercado de Motores
Vintage + Design + Food Don't Miss
Museo del Ferrocarril, Paseo de las Delicias 61 · 2nd weekend of every month · Sat 11am–10pm · Sun 11am–9pm · Free entry · Metro: Delicias (Line 3)
Over 150 artisans, designers, booksellers, artists, and vendors of gourmet products and second-hand goods gather inside the Nave Central of the Railway Museum — surrounded by historic locomotives and carriages — and across several outdoor areas with food trucks and live music. The setting is extraordinary: a 19th-century iron-and-glass train station that you can explore while shopping. Saturday hours run until 10pm, making it a full-day and evening option. One of the most genuinely magical market experiences in Madrid — Europeans compare it to the best of Berlin and London's markets.
✓ Your date: April 11–12 · Go Saturday for the full atmosphere · Combine with Sunday Rastro for a big market weekend
Malamarket
Vintage + Artisan
Plaza del Dos de Mayo, Malasaña · Saturdays during spring · Hours vary · Check @malamarket on Instagram before going
Every Saturday of spring and fall, Malamarket fills Plaza del Dos de Mayo with designers, artisans, illustrators, and vintage sellers — the hipster counterpart to El Rastro, with better prices and a more curated edit. Plus live entertainment including swing dancing classes in the square. The plaza itself is worth the trip regardless: it's the heart of Malasaña, surrounded by bars with terraces perfect for a coffee after you browse. Things to Order That You Don't Normally Get
Callos a la Madrileña
The other classic Madrid stew — beef tripe slow-cooked with chorizo and blood sausage. Rich, funky, deeply savory. The dish locals order when tourists aren't looking. Try at Taberna Antonio Sánchez or any old-school taberna.
Navajas (Razor Clams)
Madrid's secret seafood obsession. Grilled razor clams with lemon — simple and extraordinary. La Casa de las Navajas near Sol has been the city's dedicated spot since 1970.
Bocadillo de Calamares
Madrid's iconic street sandwich: crispy fried squid rings on a bread roll. Simple, cheap, and weirdly addictive. Get it near Plaza Mayor for under €4.
Croquetas de Pringá
Cocido leftovers transformed into croquettes — shredded meat and vegetables encased in creamy béchamel and fried. Malacatín is famous for theirs.
Jamón Ibérico de Bellota
Acorn-fed Iberian ham cured 3+ years. Eat it alone, sliced thin, at room temperature — not in a sandwich. Many jamonerías around Salamanca will let you sit and do a proper tasting for €8–12.
Torrijas
Spain's answer to French toast — bread soaked in milk and egg, fried, and dusted with cinnamon sugar. A Semana Santa specialty you'll find everywhere in early April. Don't skip it.

Retrieval + Recovery
Daily Morning Stretch
10–12 minutes, before screens. Floor only, no mat needed. Targeting hamstrings, hip flexors, and lower back — all connected.
Safe throughout the protocol including post-retrieval. By Day 10 you'll feel the difference in the first stretch. By Day 21 it will look different.
01
Supine Hamstring Stretch
2 MIN · 1 MIN EACH SIDE · THE MOST EFFECTIVE ONE
Lie on your back. Loop a scarf, towel, or resistance band around one foot. Extend that leg toward the ceiling, keep the other leg flat on the floor. Don't yank — find the edge of the stretch and breathe into it. Flex your foot for more intensity. Switch sides. The goal is length, not reaching a specific angle.
02
Figure-Four Hip Opener
2 MIN · 1 MIN EACH SIDE · OPENS HIP ROTATORS THAT PULL ON HAMSTRINGS
Still on your back. Cross one ankle over the opposite knee. Flex that foot (this protects the knee). Draw both legs toward your chest with your hands. You'll feel this deep in the outer hip and glute. The tighter this feels, the more your hamstrings need it — they're connected.
03
Knees-to-Chest Rock
30 SEC · LOWER BACK RESET BETWEEN SIDES
Both knees to chest. Gentle side-to-side rock. Let your lower back release against the floor. This is the transition between lying-down work and sitting work — don't skip it.
04
Seated Forward Fold
2 MIN · THE ONE MOST PEOPLE DO WRONG
Sit on the floor, legs extended in front of you, feet flexed. Sit up tall first — elongate your spine. Then hinge forward from your hips, not from your waist. Do NOT round your back to get your hands closer to your feet. A tall spine reaching forward an inch is better than a rounded back reaching all the way. Each exhale, let gravity bring you a little further. The goal is spinal length and hamstring length, not touching toes.
05
Low Lunge with Hamstring Shift
2 MIN · 1 MIN EACH SIDE · HIP FLEXOR + HAMSTRING COMBINED
Start in a low lunge (one knee on the floor, the other foot forward). Feel the hip flexor opening on the back leg — that's part 1. Then shift your hips back and straighten the front leg, keeping the front foot flexed. That's part 2 — the hamstring of the front leg lengthens. Moving between these two positions is more effective than holding either statically. This is the stretch that transfers most directly to how you walk.
06
Standing Forward Fold
1 MIN · THE FINISH · LET GRAVITY DO THE WORK
Stand, feet hip-width apart. Bend your knees as much as you need and fold forward — head completely heavy, arms hanging or crossed. Each exhale, let go a little more. You're not stretching here — you're releasing. This is the signal to your nervous system that the session is done. Slowly roll back up one vertebra at a time.
What to notice over 3 weeks Day 10 — the supine stretch: your leg goes higher with less resistance. Day 21 — the seated fold: you'll hinge noticeably further than Day 1, with a longer spine. Hamstrings respond faster than people think with daily consistency.
